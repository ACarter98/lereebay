Where Wildlife Meets the Sea

A protected bay on Guernsey's west coast, famous for birdwatching, seal spotting, and the historic L'Erée Battery.

About L'Erée Bay

L'Erée Bay offers something increasingly rare: a genuine connection with nature. This protected bay on Guernsey's rugged west coast combines dramatic coastal scenery with exceptional wildlife viewing opportunities. The bay faces the Atlantic Ocean, receiving the full force of prevailing winds and creating a wild, untamed atmosphere.

The area is designated as part of Guernsey's RAMSAR site - an internationally important wetland. The combination of salt marsh, rocky shoreline, and sandy beach creates diverse habitats that attract an impressive array of birdlife throughout the year.

Birdwatching Paradise

Bring binoculars. L'Erée is Guernsey's premier birdwatching location. Winter brings brent geese, wigeons, and teal. Spring migration sees sandpipers, plovers, and occasional rarities. The shingle bank and salt marsh provide perfect feeding grounds for wading birds at low tide.

Seal Colony

A small colony of grey seals regularly hauls out on the rocks at the bay's northern end. They're best viewed from the coastal path - approaching closely causes disturbance. Seals are present year-round but most visible during autumn breeding season.

Historic Defences

The Victorian L'Erée Battery overlooks the bay, built to defend against French invasion. It's now a peaceful ruin, home to nesting seabirds. The combination of history and nature makes this a uniquely Guernsey location.

Visitor Tips

  • Bring binoculars - essential for wildlife viewing
  • Check tide tables - birds feed at low tide
  • Park at L'Erée Battery - limited spaces
  • Stay on paths - salt marsh is fragile habitat
  • Dogs must be on leads - ground nesting birds
  • No cafes nearby - bring refreshments
